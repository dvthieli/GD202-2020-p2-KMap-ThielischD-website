FINDING YOUR WAY

...or not

A guide to wayfinding with accessible routes in mind.



Wayfinding can be defined as "the use of spatial and environmental cues to move from one place to another, supporting all types of users, regardless of their different goals within a given environment." (UNStudio Team).

There are active and passive tools to guide a user's route. Active tools are things like signage, maps, and GPS systems. Passive tools are things like environmental cues, such as foliage and how they are trimmed, sculptures and/or water features, and pathway materials.
